02The Case

Conviction cannot be bought from a desk.

Frontier markets reward conviction.

01

The information gap is structural

Public data is thin, lagging, and frequently incomplete. Diligence built on third-party reports inherits every blind spot of the stakeholders who wrote them.

02

Narrative is shaped by intermediairies

By the time a deal reaches a family office, it has passed through layers of incentive. You see the story the deal wants you to see.

03

Risk is mispriced in both directions

Headline risk inflates perceived danger while operational risk hides in plain sight. Neither is legible without being in the room.

04

Expertise is expensive to build alone

Developing real emerging-market diligence capability is costly and slow. Working collaboratively, a cohort builds it in 4 days.

03What is Last Mile?

An expedition in diligence

Last Mile brings 8–10 vetted principals into a single frontier market for four immersive days. You conduct guided due diligence on live, fundraising impact funds (financial and impact dimensions in depth), visit investee companies on the ground, and decide whether you would commit capital.

05The Diligence

Two live funds. One question: would you invest?

Fund I

Impact Fund I

A live, fundraising impact fund - diligenced end-to-end by the cohort.

01

GP Session

Half day · Manager office

Presentation and working session with the General Partner - test claims, challenge assumptions, probe the data room.

02

Portfolio Visits

Half day · On the ground

One to two investee company visits - verifying the thesis where the numbers can't: on site, with founders and operators.

03

IC session · Without the GP

A participant-led investment committee. Stepping into LP shoes, the cohort decides: would we invest - and on what terms?
